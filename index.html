There’s a simple way to not envy anyone.

Before discussing the tip, let’s look at the definition of envy:

Envy is the desire to have an attribute belonging to someone else.

Money.
Clothes.
Height.
Muscle.
Business success.
Their smile.
Their athleticism.

Envy can be dangerous.

You are focusing on someone else’s qualities and desiring them for your own.

Envy can lead to anger.
Envy can lead to bad decisions.
Envy can lead to shortcuts.

The way I avoid envy is to look at the entire person. I’d have to switch my entire person to become that person. I’d have to give up everything I had and experienced in order to be that person.

No thanks.

I’ll take my set of problems over another set of seen, and unseen, problems.
I know what my problems look like.
I know how to avoid them.

It would be nice to have a certain attribute of many different people, but I’m not willing to give up anything.

That gets me through the tough times.

Skiing and Entrepreneurship Part 1

I take a few deep breaths at the top of the mountain.
I look at my surroundings and admire nature.
I’ve mapped out the trails I want to ski.

But now that I’m here…

I could care less about the plan.
The trail other there looks exciting!

We change our course.
The other skiers agree.
We take off down the mountain.

I’m an average skier.
I don’t care if I’m the fastest down the mountain.
I’m here to enjoy it.
I may not be on a mountain again this year.

Entrepreneurship is the same journey.

We admire the view from the top.
We look at other local businesses.
We analyze the landscape of business.

We create a business plan.
It feels aggressive.
The goals feel larger than life.

Then we look at the path ahead and don’t care about what we wrote.
We're going to react to the customer, to the market.
We're going to pivot wherever we feel like pivoting.

We make sure our partners and employees are in agreement.

We don’t mind ups and downs. They happen.
We're not trying to be the fastest millionaire.

We may never open another business again.
We're here to enjoy it.

Sometimes we only focus on how fast we're going.
That’s how accidents happen in skiing and in business.
-ZG
